Sarah Battram-Cake

Summary

I started my adult life as a healthcare assistant, circled around a few different careers, had a few children but circled back to my beloved NHS to work within 111. I believe the NHS is the greatest institution in our country, of which I am proud to be a small cog. Working in the NHS has filled me with the desire to help people but also ensure that we are helping all people.

Alongside my auditing role I have an intense interest in disability inclusion, particularly non visible disabilities and neurodivergence. I have been involved with disability representation through UNISON but also through the internal staff networks. This is also reflected in my position as SEN Governor for a local infant school. We have a wealth of amazing people who are excluded from making a social impact through exclusionary practices or environments. Small changes can make huge impacts and benefit us all.
